\section{Related Work}

\subsection{Surveys and position papers}

Existing surveys offer useful partial views, but most are organized either around transport/data-movement efficiency, broad HPC storage infrastructure, or computational-storage offload. These perspectives rarely model the full architectural transition in which control authority, copy paths, and deployment topology co-evolve under GPU-dominant workloads \cite{zhang2025hpcstoragesurvey,traub2023compstoragesurvey,liu2017rackscale}. Consequently, they provide background context but leave a synthesis gap for end-to-end GPU-integrated storage design \cite{murray2021tfdata,rajbhandari2021zeroinfinity}.

\subsection{Traditional CPU-orchestrated storage systems}

Conventional POSIX-oriented and host-managed stacks place the CPU on the critical control path for submission, metadata, and completion handling. Even with optimized user-space interfaces, orchestration remains host-centric, which creates overheads that scale poorly as accelerator parallelism increases \cite{linux_io_uring,spdk_overview,nvme_spec}. For fine-grained access patterns, this control amplification can dominate available storage bandwidth and reduce effective GPU utilization \cite{murray2021tfdata,hoard2018}.

\subsection{GPU-aware and GPU-integrated storage systems}

GPU-aware file/runtime systems moved beyond host-only orchestration by exposing storage-facing abstractions to accelerator execution contexts. GPUfs and follow-up work demonstrated feasibility and highlighted consistency/granularity issues, while newer systems (e.g., GeminiFS, BaM) further push storage behavior toward GPU-centric workflows \cite{silberstein2013gpufs,shahar2016gpufs,geminifs2025,qureshi2023bam}. This line of work reframes storage as a co-evolving subsystem rather than a passive backend.

\subsection{GPU direct and bypass I/O mechanisms}

GPUDirect RDMA and GPUDirect Storage significantly improve transfer efficiency by removing host bounce buffers and reducing copies. However, they are primarily data-path optimizations; control and metadata logic often remain host-dominated, limiting end-to-end architectural change \cite{nvidia_gdrdma,nvidia_gds_blog,nvidia_gds_overview,nvidia_gds_best_practices}. Thus, bypass mechanisms are necessary building blocks but not sufficient explanations of system-level evolution \cite{pcie_spec,infiniband_spec,nvmeof_spec}.

\subsection{Storage for AI workloads}

AI training and serving expose workload properties (burstiness, phase coupling, checkpoint pressure, state growth) that are weakly captured by traditional storage tuning assumptions. Pipeline and caching studies show that ingest/prefetch/locality decisions can dominate training throughput, while memory-tiering/offload designs show tight coupling between storage policy and model scale \cite{murray2021tfdata,hoard2018,rajbhandari2021zeroinfinity}. These findings motivate architecture-level co-design across runtime, storage, and deployment layers \cite{hdf5gds2020,geminifs2025,kwon2023vllm,zhong2024distserve}.

\subsection{Disaggregated and remote storage for accelerators}

Disaggregated storage and remote-fabric access provide elasticity and pooling but introduce new tail-latency, fairness, and isolation concerns. NVMe-oF/RDMA and rack-scale studies establish the transport/deployment substrate, yet GPU-aware control placement and metadata locality remain underdeveloped \cite{nvmeof_spec,infiniband_spec,liu2017rackscale}. This creates a gap between protocol maturity and accelerator-centric orchestration needs \cite{qureshi2023bam,nvidia_gdrdma}.

\subsection{Summary and gap identification}

Across prior work, the dominant focus remains optimization of \emph{how data moves}. Far fewer studies address \emph{how control evolves}, \emph{how GPUs participate in storage decisions}, and \emph{how end-to-end semantics change across local/distributed/disaggregated deployments}. Therefore, unlike data-movement-centric surveys, our survey contributes a unified architecture-evolution framework spanning control plane, data path, and deployment model \cite{traub2023compstoragesurvey,zhang2025hpcstoragesurvey,qureshi2023bam,geminifs2025}.
