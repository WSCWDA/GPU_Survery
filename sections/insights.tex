\section{System-Level Insights}

\subsection{Bottlenecks migrate, not disappear}

Removing host copy stages shifts pressure to other components: IOMMU mappings, buffer registration caches, completion polling threads, and filesystem metadata services. Therefore, evaluation should report full critical-path decomposition instead of only aggregate bandwidth \cite{nvidia_gds_best_practices,spdk_overview}.

\subsection{Control latency dominates fine-grain I/O}

For small requests, queue management and completion signaling costs outweigh payload transfer time. GPU-driven designs gain most when they fuse request generation with compute context and minimize host wakeups \cite{bam2024,silberstein2013gpufs}.

\subsection{Concurrency must be topology-aware}

Peak throughput requires matching queue placement to PCIe hierarchy, NUMA locality, and NIC/NVMe affinity. Static ``one-size-fits-all'' queue provisioning frequently causes head-of-line blocking or CQ contention in multi-GPU servers \cite{pcie_spec,infiniband_spec}.

\subsection{Semantic gaps limit portability}

A persistent challenge is the mismatch between POSIX-style consistency expectations and accelerator-optimized asynchronous pipelines. Designs that explicitly separate metadata consistency from bulk-data movement provide more portable performance across filesystems and fabrics \cite{geminifs2025,nvme_spec}.
